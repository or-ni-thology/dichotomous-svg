# dichotomous-svg

*The Scolopacidae Construction Kit — an instrument for conceptual ornithology*

**Live:** [or-ni-thology.cloud](https://or-ni-thology.cloud/dichotomous-svg) · **Licence:** [CC0 1.0](LICENSE) — public domain, take it, change it, no credit needed

---

### Habitat & substrate

A dichotomous key is a ladder of either/ors that a naturalist climbs down until one bird remains. This instrument turns the ladder over. Rather than reading a wader down to a name, the user builds a wader up from its characters and reads off the name it cannot help but be.

Only the Scolopacidae are grown here — the sandpipers, snipe, godwits, curlews and the lone woodcock — for they are the family my dog and I most reliably confuse. No colour is required for the work: the bird is drawn as a blueprint, in skeleton ink upon a winter-sky index card, in the house manner of Opus 4.8.

### Notes on collection

The user assembles a specimen from eight characters, each named twice — once in plain English for the eye, once in the Latin of the caption beneath: *corpus* (the body), *rostrum* (the bill), *caput* (the head), *orbita* (the eye), *tarsus* (the legs), *cauda* (the tail), *locus* (the ground it stands upon), and whatever marks it wears. Each choice trades the bird a little further along the family until a plausible wader stands assembled on the card.

A bird known by its parts should be known by its egg. Press **Lay the egg** and the instrument commits: from the characters chosen it reckons the egg this bird — and only this bird — ought to lay, and hands it to the companion machine, [Ova · Transmissor](https://or-ni-thology.cloud/ova-transmissor), to be drawn and coded. Those who would test themselves may switch on **egg-verify mode**, which hides the species hints beneath each character and leaves the naturalist alone with the bird and its verdict. The finished blueprint may be collected as SVG, ready to be filed, framed, or fed back into the key.
